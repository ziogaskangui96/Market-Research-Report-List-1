<p><p>The Transistor Switching-Mode Power Supply (SMPS) market primarily features two control types: Voltage Mode Control and Current Mode Control. Voltage Mode Control regulates the output voltage by adjusting the duty cycle of the switching signal, ensuring stable voltage under varying loads. In contrast, Current Mode Control monitors the output current, providing improved response to load fluctuations and enhanced protection against overload conditions. Both modes serve diverse applications, with varying efficiencies and performance characteristics tailored to specific power supply requirements.</p></p>
